# Risk Matrix

Use this matrix to turn fuzzy copyright anxiety into a concrete action.

## Severity Levels

### Red

Do not publish or deliver until changed, authorized, or reviewed by counsel.

Typical red signals:
- The work is clearly recognizable as a specific protected character, film/game/anime frame, logo, mascot, or artwork.
- A brand, IP owner, celebrity, musician, or rights holder would be the main reason the work has value.
- The work uses a known singer/actor/celebrity voice, AI cover, face swap, or persona without authorization.
- A commercial, sponsored, client, paid-course, marketplace, merchandise, or ad use relies on unlicensed music/IP/personality rights.
- The user cannot show any license for BGM, stock footage, templates, or client-provided assets used in a paid project.
- The prompt or brief explicitly says to generate a named protected character, celebrity, living artist imitation, or branded ad look.

Recommended action: stop, replace, de-identify, buy/obtain a license, ask the client for written warranties, or consult a lawyer.

### Yellow

Publish only after modification, documentation, or scope reduction.

Typical yellow signals:
- It evokes a genre or style but includes some distinctive elements that might point to one specific IP.
- The work is non-commercial but public and likely to attract fan/IP audience attention.
- Music comes from a platform library but the use is commercial, cross-platform, sponsored, or outside the platform.
- The user has a license but not for the exact territory, duration, media, or commercial use.
- A real person is not named but the generated face/voice is close enough to invite comparison.
- The work is a critique, review, parody, or education piece, but the amount copied may exceed what is needed.

Recommended action: reduce similarity, add original expression, replace questionable assets, document sources, add attribution where appropriate, and avoid monetization if authorization is unclear.

### Green

Relatively low risk, but keep records.

Typical green signals:
- Original character/world with no recognizable protected elements.
- Generic genre influence only: cyberpunk, wuxia, noir, watercolor, 1990s TV look, etc.
- Licensed stock/music/assets with terms matching the exact planned use.
- Platform-native tools/assets used within the platform and within stated permissions.
- Public-domain or creator-owned material, with provenance recorded.

Recommended action: publish with records retained.

## Risk Escalators

Increase severity when:
- The use is commercial, sponsored, client-facing, or monetized.
- The post includes product links, course sales, paid downloads, or lead generation.
- The IP/person/music is famous or aggressively enforced.
- The work is highly similar in more than one dimension: name + look + relationship + voice + setting.
- The user plans repeated/batch production.
- The output could substitute for the original market.

## Risk Reducers

Decrease severity only when evidence exists:
- Written license or client warranty covers the exact use.
- Strong transformation and original expression are visible.
- The use is narrow commentary, criticism, news, teaching, or research.
- The copied amount is minimal and necessary.
- The work avoids logos, names, signature costumes, signature voices, and distinctive protected expressions.

Do not call something safe merely because it is AI-generated.
