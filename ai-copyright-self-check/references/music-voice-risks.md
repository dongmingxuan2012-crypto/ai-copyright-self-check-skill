# Music / BGM / Voice Risk

Use this reference for videos, ads, AI covers, voice cloning, platform music libraries, soundalikes, and any work with audio.

## Music Rights Are Layered

A track may involve:
- Composition rights: melody and lyrics.
- Sound recording rights: the specific recording/master.
- Performance rights.
- Synchronization use: pairing music with video.
- Platform-specific library terms.

Getting one layer does not automatically clear the others.

## Common Red Flags

Rate Red when:
- A commercial/sponsored/client video uses a popular song without a clear commercial sync license.
- The user uses a platform trending sound outside that platform or in a paid ad.
- AI cloning imitates a known singer, actor, creator, or celebrity voice.
- AI cover content uses a recognizable song and recognizable celebrity voice.
- A voice model was trained from someone else's voice without permission.
- The user cannot identify the BGM source for a client deliverable.

## Common Yellow Flags

Rate Yellow when:
- A platform music library is used, but the post is sponsored or cross-posted.
- The user has "royalty-free" music but the license does not clearly cover ads, paid courses, or client use.
- The music is AI-generated but sounds like a famous song, artist, or hook.
- The voice is "inspired by" a famous performer, even if not named.

## Safer Options

Recommend:
- Use explicitly commercial-licensed music.
- Use original commissioned or self-generated music with terms saved.
- Keep platform-library music inside the platform and inside allowed use.
- Replace celebrity voice with a generic original voice.
- Store license screenshots, receipts, terms, and project files.

## Audio Review Limits

Do not claim certain identification unless the user provides a track name, audio/video file, platform link, or reliable metadata. If identification is uncertain, say so and recommend conservative replacement or licensing.

For legal/publication claims about active music lawsuits, recent settlements, platform terms, or statutory rules, verify current sources first.
